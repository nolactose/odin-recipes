Chocolate Chip Cookies

Nothing better than homemade chocolate chip cookies!

From what little I know about baking, it's important to carefully follow the directions; one deviation could have a very drastic impact on the results!

I personally don't like my cookies to be super sweet, so I dial back on the amount of sugar added. This way, I don't feel as guilty when devouring an ungodly number of cookies in one sitting

Ingredients
  • 3/4 cup of butter (salted or unsalted, room temperature)
  • 1/2 cup white (granulated) sugar
  • 3/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 cups all purpose flour (unbleached)
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 (half) tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt (or can use sea salt if you like chunky salt)
  • 2 cups of semi-sweet chocolate morsels (or chopped up chocolate)
Steps
  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F. Line a baking pan with parchment paper, or aluminum foil (non-stick spray optional)
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the dry ingredients (minus the sugar):
    • baking powder
    • baking soda>
    • salt
    • flour (when measuring out, DO NOT PACK OR STUFF DOWN INTO THE MEASURING CUP
  3. In another bowl, beat together the butter, white sugar, brown sugar and vanilla extract until creamy
  4. Mix in the eggs one at a time, making sure the egg is properly mixed in after each addition
  5. Mix in the dry ingredients (the flour mixture) in increments until the cookie dough is homogenous
  6. Evenly mix in chocolate chip morsels
  7. Roll 2-3 tbsps of cookie dough (or however big/small you want them to be) into small spheres before placing onto prepared baking sheets
  8. Bake in preheated oven for 8-10 minutes. Take out when they are just barely starting to turn golden brown
  9. Let sit on baking sheet for 2 minutes before moving them to a cooling rack
  10. Enjoy!
